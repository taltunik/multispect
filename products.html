Products · One Instrument, Three Layers

Three products. One spectrum.

Each product spans the whole civic–security scale — they differ in depth and cadence, not in coverage. Enter at any layer; move between them. The same intelligence is then delivered at three command altitudes.

Layer 1 · The Method

The Polygon — full-spectrum mapping.

We define the Area of Responsibility — a district, a city, or a specific neighbourhood — and map the entire ecosystem of voices, places and institutions before a single reading goes live.

Digital

Relevant local Telegram channels, WhatsApp groups, TikTok trends, Facebook community pages, and local news forums.

Human

Clerics, educators, business and community leaders, neighbourhood elders and key voices — anyone who can move opinion within the polygon.

Institutional

Religious, educational, charitable and civic bodies; student unions and political cells; and the informal power structures and funding networks that shape the ground.

Layer 2 · The Deep-Dive

The Deep-Dive Report — a strategic study, on the table.

A one-time, in-depth read of how the polygon is changing — commissioned when a client needs to understand a dynamic before acting on it. Broad, civic-leaning, strategic.

Example study types · civic-first, spanning the spectrum

Cultural · Identity

Youth identity & belonging trends.

How a generation defines itself — and where it diverges from community elders.

Social · Civic

Trust in public institutions.

Where confidence in services, authority and local government is rising or eroding.

Religious · Cultural

Dynamics around a flashpoint.

How a specific event reverberates through religious and cultural networks.

Security · The Edge

Drivers of radicalisation in District X.

The same depth, applied at the far end — what pulls a few toward the extreme.
